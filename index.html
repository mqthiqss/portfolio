Who am I?

My name is Mathias Culot and I am a twenty year old student at HELHa, where I'm pursuing a degree in Industrial IT. I've always been passionate about new technologies. I chose these studies because I think that thanks to the field of computing, we'll soon be making the greatest advances.

I'm someone who likes to get things done early, always taking the first step to solve problems or tasks. Whether it's starting a new project or finding ways to make things better, being proactive helps me stay on top of things. If I'm asked to complete a task in a two-week period, I know that I'll feel better if I start doing it straight away. That way, I won't be surprised when I receive other, more urgent tasks.

I'm good at being diplomatic, which means I try to be careful and polite when I talk to people. I think about how others feel and what they think, I always try to make communication smoother and more positive.

Patience is another thing I'm proud of. I stay calm and thoughtful when things get tricky, whether I'm helping someone learn or dealing with a problem. For example, I remember my C programming exam in 1st year. The instructions were really hard and I could hear all my classmates stressing and trying several solutions directly on the coding programme. Personally, I decided to calm down and take a sheet of paper to analyse the statement first and then, once I thought I'd found the solution, I started typing my code. This method saved me a lot of time and I passed the exam.

I'm a talkative person, and I like to chat and share ideas with others. It brings energy and fun to different situations.

Yet, like everyone, I have my not-so-great parts. I like to stick to a plan and have a fixed schedule for work. While it helps me stay organised, I'm also learning to be more flexible. For an example, one of the things I perhaps didn't like about my second-year project was that we were totally free in terms of our progress. We only had a deadline where everything had to be finished, but there were no regular reports to hand in or anything like that. This was an added source of stress because I had no way of making sure we were on schedule.

Sometimes, I find it hard to stay focused. For example, when I'm coding a program I might wonder about its efficiency, look to see if there are any other solutions and end up getting lost in the language documentation. I know this, and I'm working on a plan to get better at concentrating and staying productive.

My Experience

In my second year of studies, I embarked on a full-stack project. This project involved the automation of an industrial process, which I found both very enlightening. Our task was to develop a website interfacing with an Arduino embedded system, measuring the speed of a passing brick on a conveyor. This demanded a combination of software development, hardware integration, and data analysis skills.

The most difficult part was the embedded system, where I worked on prototyping and accurately displaying measurements on a screen and the website, also implementing a calibration process. The calibration process consisted of doing measures over and over while adjusting the sensors sensitivity.

We only had seven days available at school, so we also had to work at the house. Working remotely was difficult because we had limited access to the conveyor and sensors, putting us behind schedule. However, I made up for lost time by fixing all the issues with the website at home so that we had more time at school to work on the embedded system.

Despite stress, the project was rewarding. I navigated unknown errors, developed my skills, and thanks to the independence encouraged by our teachers, the accomplishment of this project felt truly satisfying. I also sharpened my ability to effectively share ideas and collaborate with peers and mentors, recognizing the significance of clear and concise communication.



I'm also going to do a work placement at Sonaca, a company that makes various parts of aircraft wings in Gosselies. My role will be to fully develop a non-conformance management application. I'll have to develop this application using the Mendix low-code surface and I'll also have to use my knowledge of Java.

Moreover, I am comfortable with C and C++ programming languages, essential for developing efficient softwares. These skills enable me to create low-level code for specialized industrial applications. My experience with C++ has enabled me to create a lot of embedded system programmes, for example.



Also, my expertise in Java programming allow me to develop cross-platform applications and web solutions, crucial in today's interconnected world.



In addition, I know how to make scripts on Linux thanks to the Bash programming language that I've used throughout my studies. This language is very useful for automating redundant tasks as part of system administration.



However, my skills are not limited to mastering a few programming languages. I'm able to create and manage databases, making sure that the data is easily accessible. I'm also fairly comfortable with embedded systems. I've often had to work with electricity, sensors and actuators, so my knowledge isn't limited to software.



I've also had several courses on PLCs (Programmable Logic Controllers), and in particular I've learnt to develop using 'Ladder Logic', which is a graphical programming language used by many PLCs.



I also used docker a lot in 2nd and 3rd year. Docker is a lightweight virtualisation platform for creating and deploying applications in containers. Containers encapsulate applications and their dependencies. This ensures portability and consistency of execution between environments. This simplifies deployment and facilitates collaboration between teams.



Finally, I bring valuable experience in system administration, involving the management and maintenance of computer systems and networks to ensure their stability and security.
